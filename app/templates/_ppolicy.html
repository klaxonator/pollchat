General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR)

For the purposes of the European General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), we are a data controller of your information.

Our aim is to minimize the collection of personally identifiable information. However, for the purposes of the GDPR (a law we generally think is a pretty good idea, despite the headaches associated with documents such as this one), the legal basis for Mayfly Research's collection and use of the personal information described in this privacy policy depends on the specific personal information being collected and the specific context in which we collect the information. Such permissible contexts include:

  • Mayfly Research needs to perform a contract with you.
  • You have given Mayfly Research permission to collect and use the information.
  • Processing your personal information is in Mayfly Research's legitimate interests.
  • Mayfly Research needs to comply with the law.

Mayfly Research will retain your personal information only for as long as is necessary for the purposes set out in this Privacy Policy. We will retain and use your information to the extent necessary to comply with our legal obligations, resolve disputes, and enforce our policies.

If you are a resident of the European Economic Area (EEA), you have certain data protection rights. If you wish to be informed what personal information we hold about you, or if you wish such information to be removed from our systems, please contact us. Also, please note that your personal information (as described in this privacy policy) will be transferred to, processed by, and stored on our servers in the United States.

In certain circumstances, you have the following data protection rights:

THIRD PARTY CONTENT AND PRIVACY POLICIES

Twitter content

Twitter is a microblogging service of Twitter Inc., 1355 Market Street, Suite 900, San Francisco, CA 94103, USA (hereinafter “Twitter”).

As the aim of this site is to allow users to understand trends and behavior associated with election-related content on Twitter, we feel it is necessary to provide actual examples of Twitter content. Under the terms of Twitter's developer rules, we are required to display their content in a certain branded format. In order to ensure this content is dynamic and up-to-date, we use their "embedded Tweet" function to display active Tweets.

While this has certain conveniences, it also exposes our visitors to a certain amount of online tracking.

To the best of our knowledge, when you visit a page on our website that contains embedded Twitter content, your browser will directly connect to Twitter’s servers, where the embedded Tweet is loaded from. The fact that you have visited our online services will be transmitted to Twitter (including information on your IP address) even if you are not logged into your account. If you are logged into your account, Twitter may be able to associate your visit to our pages with your user account. As the provider of the Pollchatter site, we are not aware of the full content of this transmitted data, and are not cognizant of how this information will be used by Twitter.

We strongly recommend you visit Twitter's privacy policy for more information on this subject. A key element (but not the only relevant element) in their policy is the following:

When you view our content on third-party websites that integrate Twitter content such as embedded timelines or Tweet buttons, we may receive Log Data that includes the web page you visited. We use this information to better understand the use of our services, to protect the safety and integrity of our platform, and to show more relevant content, including ads. We do not associate this web browsing history with your name, email address, phone number, or username, and we delete, obfuscate, or aggregate it after no longer than 30 days. We do not collect this data from browsers that we believe to be located in the European Union or EFTA States.

We recognize that even this information can be difficult to understand. We highly recommend visiting pro-privacy organizations such as the Electronic Frontier Foundation, whose Privacy Badger tool does a very good job with regard to protecting you against rampant online tracking.
